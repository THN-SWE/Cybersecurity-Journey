- Permissions determine the ways different users can interact with a file or directory.

![](../Assets/Screenshot%202026-02-25%20174527.png)

- [[../Misc/Cyber Security/Linux/Permission-types]]
- the first `-` is filetype which is in this case is normal file. 
- then the permissions, broken into three sets of thre characters. 

> The first set of permissions are for the owner who owns the file. 
> `-rw` means owner can read and write the file. p
> **other permissions will have no effect even if you are the owner only permissions for owner may apply same goes for other sets. **

> Second set of permission is for group that owns the file. if you are not a owner and member of the group then these permissions will apply for you. 
> `-r-`

> Last one is for anyone else, who is not owner nor a member of group the file belongs to.. 
> `r--`
