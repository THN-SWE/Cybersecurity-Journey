# 🛡️ Cybersecurity Domains — Scenarios Breakdown

---

### **1. Security and Risk Management**

**Definition:**  
Defines security goals and objectives, risk mitigation, compliance, business continuity, and laws.

**💡 Example:**

> A retail company wants to launch an online payment system.  
> Before launching, the security team performs a **risk assessment** and finds that customer card data could be stolen during payment processing.  
> They **encrypt payment data**, create a **data breach response plan**, and **train employees** on handling sensitive info.  
> ✅ _That’s risk management and compliance in practice._

---

### **2. Asset Security**

**Definition:**  
Protects physical and digital assets, including data storage, maintenance, retention, and destruction.

**💡 Example:**

> A hospital stores patient records both digitally and in print.  
> They **encrypt digital records**, **lock paper files** in secure cabinets, and **delete old records** after 7 years following policy.  
> ✅ _That’s asset security — protecting information throughout its lifecycle._

---

### **3. Security Architecture and Engineering**

**Definition:**  
Designs and implements layered, effective systems and tools to protect data and infrastructure.

**💡 Example:**

> An engineer builds an internal HR system using **firewalls**, **IDS/IPS**, and **least privilege access**.  
> The design ensures that if one system is breached, others remain unaffected.  
> ✅ _That’s secure architecture — resilience by design._

---

### **4. Communication and Network Security**

**Definition:**  
Secures data during transmission across wired and wireless networks.

**💡 Example:**

> Remote workers connect to office Wi-Fi.  
> The IT team sets up **VPNs**, uses **WPA3 encryption**, and applies **network segmentation** to isolate sensitive departments.  
> ✅ _That’s communication and network security — keeping traffic private and controlled._

---

### **5. Identity and Access Management (IAM)**

**Definition:**  
Controls user identities and access to systems, both physical and digital.

**💡 Example:**

> When an employee leaves, the IAM team **revokes access**, **disables accounts**, and enforces **multi-factor authentication (MFA)** for everyone else.  
> They also use **role-based access control (RBAC)** to limit access by job function.  
> ✅ _That’s IAM — ensuring the right people access the right things._

---

### **6. Security Assessment and Testing**

**Definition:**  
Conducts vulnerability testing, audits, and assessments to identify and fix weaknesses.

**💡 Example:**

> Before releasing a web app, the company hires a **penetration tester** who runs **vulnerability scans**, **manual exploits**, and **log reviews**.  
> They find and fix SQL injection flaws before launch.  
> ✅ _That’s proactive testing — find holes before hackers do._

---

### **7. Security Operations**

**Definition:**  
Focuses on incident detection, response, investigation, and daily monitoring.

**💡 Example:**

> The SOC receives an alert that an employee’s account is sending suspicious emails.  
> Analysts **investigate logs**, **quarantine the device**, and **update filters** to block phishing attempts.  
> ✅ _That’s security operations — keeping systems safe 24/7._

---

### **8. Software Development Security**

**Definition:**  
Applies secure coding practices to ensure applications are built and maintained securely.

**💡 Example:**

> A developer accidentally stores passwords in plain text.  
> The security team catches it during a **code review**, teaches them to use **bcrypt hashing**, and adds **static code analysis tools** to the workflow.  
> ✅ _That’s software development security — building safe code from day one._
